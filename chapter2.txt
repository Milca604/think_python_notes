Chapter 2 Variables, expressions and statements.

2.1	Values and types
	Value: 1, 2, ‘Hello, World!’
	Type: 2 is integer, ‘Hello, World!’ is a string (identified with quotation marks).
		>>> type(‘Hello, World!’)
		<class ‘str’>
		>>> type(17)
		<class ‘int’>	-denotes integer (no decimal point)	
		>>> type(3.2)
		<class ‘float’>	-denotes floating points (with decimal point)
